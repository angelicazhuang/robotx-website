FRC 2019 Deep Space

In 2019’s Deep Space FRC season, RobotX began the season with a year of experience, twice the team members, two more mentors, and still barely an idea in our collective heads. The first 2 weeks were spent designing, calculating, realizing the calculations were wrong, and redesigning. After the 2.5 week mark, we decided that we’ve stalled enough and it was time to start building. The design was highly reminiscent of our 2018 robot, albeit with a significantly longer arm. The general game plan before we saw other teams was to fill up the rocket with hatches. As we got to the competition, we slowly realized that that was the exact opposite of the correct game plan. Thankfully, we made our robot adaptable, such that we can take on almost any role. We fought our way through our qualifying matches, ranking anywhere from 5th to 25th. We ended the qualifiers in 16th place overall, making it likely that a top 8 team would pick us. And they did! On the 5th seed alliance, we were the only lower-seed team to win a match in the quarterfinals. This year, we took home the Team Spirit award, and we’re looking for more next year!

About the Robot

Red EVA was tactfully designed by our team to manuver milk crates. We decided to use an arm design becuase of its robustness and its smiplicity to build. Our robot was easily servicable and was efficient at completing a multitude of jobs manuvering milk crates efficiently near the ground. In order to manipulate the game pieces, we have a gripper that uses compliance wheels to intake the milk crate and can hold it while in the air. This design has been very efficient and works on a variety of different objects.
